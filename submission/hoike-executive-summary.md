# Hōʻike Executive Summary

## The Opportunity

Hōʻike is a Hawaiʻi-centered AI pathway navigator designed for HIDOE middle and high school students. It addresses a problem that sits underneath the state's broader brain-drain challenge: many students do not just lack access to opportunity, they lack access to the unwritten scripts that make opportunity legible. They may hear about careers, pathways, and networking, but not know how those things connect to their classes, communities, or next steps.

Hōʻike responds by unhiding the hidden curriculum of career navigation. When students can translate what they are already doing in school into stories about strengths, interests, and ways of giving back, and when those futures are introduced by real people from Hawaiʻi, local pathways become more visible, more desirable, and more attainable.

## What the Product Does

Hōʻike combines three ideas in one protected system:

- `Curriculum Translation`: a `Professional Why` and `Skills to Stories` layer explain why a school assignment matters, what it reveals about a student's strengths, and how those strengths might serve Hawaiʻi.
- `Local Voices`: moderated profiles and short intro videos let students meet local professionals in a school-safe environment that feels like a protected LinkedIn for exploration.
- `Small Steps`: every pathway ends with realistic actions such as saving a pathway, drafting a question, talking with a counselor, joining a club, or trying a mini project.

Students are not pushed into adult-style networking. Instead, they use a guided `Ask a Question` flow with prompt starters that lower the barrier to outreach:

- `What should I try in high school if I am curious about this field?`
- `What made you choose this work?`
- `What surprised you about this job?`
- `What is one small step I could take this month?`

## Why It Fits Hawaiʻi

Hawaiʻi's education strategy already emphasizes graduates who are `Globally Competitive, Locally Committed`, while HIDOE's Career and Technical Education framework emphasizes preparing students to live and thrive in Hawaiʻi through high-skill, high-wage, high-demand careers. Hōʻike supports that direction by helping younger students understand what those futures actually look like before they exit the local pipeline.

This timing also fits broader institutional momentum. The University of Hawaiʻi Community Colleges System's 2026 RFP for an AI-integrated career pathways platform shows that Hawaiʻi is already moving toward AI-supported pathway infrastructure. Hōʻike complements that work by focusing on the earliest part of the journey: visibility, confidence, and guided exploration for younger students. It also pushes back against a common social script in peripheral places: that leaving is the clearest sign of ambition while staying or returning must be defended. Hōʻike reframes local contribution as an ambitious choice too.

## Why Codex and Why Handshake

OpenAI Codex is the right fit because Hōʻike needs a bounded AI layer that can translate assignment context into pathway relevance, scaffold question prompts, and provide age-appropriate next steps. The AI is not replacing counselors or mentors. It is making local futures easier to recognize and explore.

Handshake matters in two ways:

- now, as the showcase layer for presenting the concept and demonstrating its relevance to career exploration
- later, as a bridge into the college-to-career ecosystem once students age into internships, postsecondary pathways, and early-career visibility

## Safety by Design

Hōʻike is pseudonymous by default. A student might appear as `HappyCow01` and provide only self-entered interests, goals, and constraints needed for guidance. Professionals do not see student legal names, and there are no open inboxes or unsupervised employer contacts in the MVP. At the same time, authorized school staff can resolve misuse if accountability is necessary.

This model lowers the social risk for students while also protecting participating professionals. A later-phase enhancement is a guided student snapshot built from interests, hobbies, causes, skills, and fields of curiosity, but not public-facing school names.

## The Roadmap

`Phase 1: Browser MVP`

- lightweight overlay or demo environment
- core features: `Professional Why`, `Hawaiʻi Pathway Exploration`, `Local Voices`, `Ask a Question`, and `Small Steps`
- no real-name requirement, no open messaging

`Phase 2: School-Sanctioned Pilot`

- educator dashboard
- moderated question workflows
- approved professional participation
- assignment and advisory integration
- teacher-managed cohort spaces such as `PenguinPod`
- student voting to invite selected professionals into classroom discussions

`Phase 3: Statewide and Postsecondary Bridge`

- deeper links to local employers, alumni, and sector partnerships
- bridges into dual-credit, UH pathways, internships, and eventual Handshake visibility

## Why This Matters

Hōʻike does not claim to solve Hawaiʻi's brain drain alone. It addresses one piece of the problem that is frequently overlooked: the gap between opportunity existing and opportunity feeling reachable. By unhiding the hidden curriculum of career navigation, Hōʻike gives students a clearer sense of how to build a future in Hawaiʻi before leaving starts to feel like the only legible option.
